At the Market

Learn essential English phrases for shopping at the market. Whether you're asking about prices, looking for items, or making a purchase, we've got you covered!

Key Vocabulary

  • How much is this?: A common question to ask about the price of an item.
  • I’ll take it: A phrase used when you want to buy something.
  • Can you give me a discount?: A question asking for a lower price.
  • Do you have...?: Used when asking for a specific item (e.g., "Do you have apples?").
  • Where can I find...?: Used to ask for the location of items in the market.

Sample Dialogues

Customer: How much is this watermelon?

Seller: It’s $5 for one.

Customer: Can you give me a discount?

Seller: I can lower the price to $4 for you.

Customer: Great! I’ll take it.

Customer: Do you have any bananas?

Seller: Yes, they are on the next stall.

Cultural Context

Tip: In many markets, especially in places like India, bargaining is common. You can often negotiate the price with the seller, especially for items like fruits and vegetables.

Tip: In other countries, markets may have fixed prices, and asking for a discount might be less common. It’s always good to ask politely.
