    What are Nouns?

    A noun is a word that names a person, place, thing, or idea. Nouns are fundamental in forming sentences and are categorized into different types based on their usage.

    Types of Nouns:

    • Common Nouns: These refer to general items rather than specific ones. Examples: dog, city, book.
    • Proper Nouns: These name specific people, places, or things. Always capitalized. Examples: India, Shakespeare, Eiffel Tower.
    • Concrete Nouns: These are things you can perceive with your senses. Examples: flower, car, music.
    • Abstract Nouns: These represent ideas, qualities, or feelings. Examples: love, freedom, happiness.
    • Collective Nouns: These describe groups or collections. Examples: team, herd, jury.

    Examples in Sentences:

    1. The dog barked loudly. (Common Noun)
    2. Paris is a beautiful city. (Proper Noun)

    What are Pronouns?

    A pronoun is a word used to replace a noun to avoid repetition and simplify sentences. They are essential for effective communication.

    Types of Pronouns:

    • Subject Pronouns: Replace the subject of the sentence. Examples: I, you, he, she, we, they.
    • Object Pronouns: Replace the object of the sentence. Examples: me, him, her, us, them.
    • Possessive Pronouns: Indicate ownership. Examples: mine, yours, his, hers, ours, theirs.
    • Reflexive Pronouns: Refer back to the subject. Examples: myself, yourself, himself.
    • Demonstrative Pronouns: Point to specific things. Examples: this, that, these, those.

    Examples in Sentences:

    1. She loves reading books. (Subject Pronoun)
    2. This book is mine. (Possessive Pronoun)
