What is a Tense?

A tense in English grammar expresses the time when an action or event happens. There are three main tenses: Present, Past, and Future. Each tense has four subtypes, resulting in a total of 12 tenses.

Learn about the 12 types of tenses in English grammar.

1. Present Simple

Use: For actions that are habitual or general truths.

Example: I play football every day.

2. Present Continuous

Use: For actions happening now or around the present time.

Example: She is reading a book.

3. Present Perfect

Use: For actions that happened at an unspecified time and are still relevant.

Example: I have finished my homework.

4. Present Perfect Continuous

Use: For actions that started in the past and are still continuing.

Example: I have been studying for two hours.

5. Past Simple

Use: For actions that happened and finished in the past.

Example: He visited London last year.

6. Past Continuous

Use: For actions that were in progress at a specific time in the past.

Example: They were watching TV at 8 PM.

7. Past Perfect

Use: For actions that were completed before another action in the past.

Example: I had already eaten when she arrived.

8. Past Perfect Continuous

Use: For actions that were ongoing in the past before another action.

Example: He had been working for 2 hours before the meeting started.

9. Future Simple

Use: For actions that will happen in the future.

Example: I will go to the park tomorrow.

10. Future Continuous

Use: For actions that will be in progress at a specific time in the future.

Example: I will be traveling next week.

11. Future Perfect

Use: For actions that will be completed before a specific point in the future.

Example: By 2025, I will have graduated.

12. Future Perfect Continuous

Use: For actions that will be ongoing in the future until a certain point.

Example: By next month, she will have been studying for three years.
