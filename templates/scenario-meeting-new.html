Meeting Someone New

Learn how to introduce yourself and start conversations with new people in English.

Key Vocabulary

  • Hello: A greeting used when meeting someone.
  • Nice to meet you: A polite phrase used after introductions.
  • Where are you from?: A question used to ask about someone's origin.
  • What do you do?: A question used to ask about someone's profession or occupation.
  • How are you?: A common greeting used to ask about someone's well-being.

Sample Dialogues

Person 1: Hi! My name is John. What's your name?

Person 2: Hello, John! I'm Sarah. Nice to meet you!

Person 1: Nice to meet you too! Where are you from?

Person 2: I'm from New York. How about you?

Person 1: I'm from London. What do you do?

Person 2: I'm a teacher. And you?

Person 1: I'm a software engineer. That's great!

Interactive Practice

Question: What would you say if someone asks "Where are you from?"

Cultural Context

Tip: In many Western cultures, people greet each other with a handshake or a hug, depending on the level of familiarity. In other cultures, bowing or offering a slight nod is common.

Tip: When you meet someone for the first time, it's polite to ask about their job or where they are from as a conversation starter.
